I keep highlighting things and think, why didn't anyone explain it this way in class. It makes me want to actually learn this properly. It reminds me that there are people out there who care about things and that gives me some hope about the field I'm going into.

Watched Gone Girl today and loved it. That's my backup plan: if I ever have to marry someone, I'll fake my own death and start a new life in Iran. Find a small apartment near a bookshop in Tehran, read Forough all day, drink tea and let the world forget I existed. Amy had the right idea, just the wrong country.

I've been thinking about death lately. Not in a dark way and I think it's a good concept and why people look at it in a bad way. What happens to a person's privacy once they're gone? When someone dies, people go through their phone, their journals, their messages, their photos. Everything that was private suddenly belongs to everyone else. And nobody asks permission because there's no one left to ask. It bothers me. When you're alive, your thoughts are yours. Your conversations are yours. The things you wrote at 3am that you never meant anyone to see those are yours. But the moment you stop existing, all of that becomes someone else's property. Your family reads your diary. Your friends scroll through your chat history. Strangers decide what to keep and what to throw away.

And the body, is it still you? Or is it just what's left after you've already gone? If a person is truly gone, if there's nothing left of them in that body, then what are we preserving? What are we burying?

I don't have answers. I just think about it. What would I want someone to do with my things if I wasn't here anymore? Delete everything, probably. And don't make a museum out of someone who wanted to be forgotten.
